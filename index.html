About

Red foxes are the most common of the Carnivora members. They have the ability to adapt quickly to the enviroment leading to them to be ranked among of the top 100 invasive species. They are mostly nocturnal creatures that sleep underground with usally a pair or small group of families. They cane be found at sealevel and 4,5000 meters in elevation. They are able to hear low-frequency and smell animals digging underground to catch prey. Prey is usually small rodents but they are omnivores with a highly varied diet that consists of mice, raccons, raaspberies, and acorns to name a few. They are know to consume over 300 animal species and few dozen species of plants.
